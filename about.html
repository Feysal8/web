WHY FREE

English should be considered "free" because it serves as a global lingua franca, facilitating international communication in business, diplomacy, and culture. Making English readily accessible promotes educational opportunities, enabling learners worldwide to acquire valuable language skills without barriers. This accessibility fosters cultural exchange, allowing diverse ideas, literature, and art to be shared across borders, which enhances creativity and understanding. Additionally, proficiency in English can boost economic growth by opening job opportunities, while free resources help reduce the digital divide in an increasingly online world. Promoting social equity through free language education ensures that individuals from all socioeconomic backgrounds can participate fully in society, engage in civic matters, and make informed decisions. Overall, advocating for free access to English creates a more inclusive, educated, and connected global community.
